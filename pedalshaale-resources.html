Participant Training Process

  • Theoretical component 2 hours
  • Theoretical training focuses on learning the traffic and cycling rules identifying dangerous situations.
  • A detailed document for the student/commuter to navigate on the streets of Bangalore.
  • The document also consists of questions back for the student to practise before the test.
  • The document will be followed by an online test where their understanding of traffic rules will be tested.

  • Practical component 8 hours minimum
  • Practical training will prepare you for cycling on the road.
  • Aimed at obtaining proper control over the cycle and learning how to manoeuvre in traffic.
  • Practical training will be taught by a certified trainer or Instructor.
  • The practical test will be organised and supervised by a certified instructor to ensure quality and safety.

Level 1

Teaches trainees to learn to control and master a cycle. The training takes place in an environment away from cars or traffic – usually in a playground or closed park.

Level 2

Is delivered on local roads giving trainees real cycling experience that equips them with skills to deal with traffic on short journeys such as cycling to school, work or the local shops.

Level 3

Teaches the skills to tackle a wider variety of traffic conditions than Level 2. It is designed to equip trainees with skills that enable them to be able to deal with all types of road conditions and more challenging traffic situations.

About

This initiative is under an MoU between Karnataka Non-Motorised Transport Agency (KNMTA), Karnataka Bicycle Dealers Association(KBDA) and Citizens for SUstainability (CiFoS). The target is to train as many people as possible in Karnataka to learn to cycle confidently on Indian streets. The training program will be run across the city near stores of KBDA members.
