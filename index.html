The Challenge We Face

1.5 Lakh Road Deaths Annually in India

60% are pedestrians and cyclists — the most vulnerable road users who deserve the highest protection.

Despite being the majority of road users in many cities, pedestrians and cyclists lack basic infrastructure, legal protections, and enforcement mechanisms. The National Active Mobility Bill aims to change this.

What the Bill Promises

Three transformative commitments for Indian cities

Safe Streets

Dedicated pedestrian infrastructure with complete, continuous, and safe footpaths. Protected crossings, barrier-free design, and street-level enforcement to ensure safe passage for all pedestrians.

Cycling Infrastructure

Protected bicycle lanes separated from motor traffic, secure parking facilities at transit stations and public places, and connected cycling networks across neighborhoods.

15-Minute City

Local access to essential services within a short walk or bicycle ride. Mixed-use neighborhoods with shops, schools, parks, and transit - reducing dependence on motorized vehicles.
